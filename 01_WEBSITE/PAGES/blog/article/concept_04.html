Federal lab inspections: the cheat sheet.

By Enduro Field Team · September 2025

Federal lab inspections aren't harder than provincial ones — they're more specific. The auditor walks in with a list, and the list always wins. Our job, as the GC, is to make sure the list and the building agree before the auditor arrives.

What auditors actually check.

Most of the time, it's the basics — clearances, signage, eyewash distances, fume hood face velocities, segregation of flammables. The 'specialty' items get the attention in the brief, but it's the unsexy basics that catch most teams.

We keep a one-page punch list per inspection type, updated after every audit. It lives in the project handover packet. The PM reviews it at 50% and 100% complete walks, before the inspector is invited.

Three patterns from the last 12 federal inspections.

Pattern one: signage. Always signage. Spec the signs in shop drawings, install before final cleanup, photograph in place.

Pattern two: face velocities. Get them certified twice — once at install, once two weeks before audit. They drift.

Pattern three: gas line labels. Every line, every valve, every tee. Use the building's labeling scheme, not the trade's default.

The handover packet.

Our compliance handover includes: as-built drawings, commissioning reports, certifications, and the one-page punch list with date-stamped photos. The auditor sees the packet before the walk. It pre-answers most of their list.

Note · 01Federal scope here = CFIA, BSL-2 research, FDA-equivalent inspections. Provincial scope differs.
Note · 02Our internal pre-audit checklist is 47 items. Available on request.
Note · 03"Drift" on face velocities is real. The HVAC settles within the first month and the readings change.
Note · 04The one-page punch list is the most useful piece of paper on a lab build. Worth printing twice.
