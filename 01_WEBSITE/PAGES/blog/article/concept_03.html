Pre-fab walls.
16-week civic build.

A look inside Ray Perrault Fieldhouse, where off-site assembly compressed a 22-week schedule into 16 — without trading away the finish.

Frame 01

The site. A municipal park, league soccer four nights a week, kids' games on Saturdays. The fieldhouse needed to land without taking the park offline.

Why pre-fab.

Most public-park builds eat their lunch on weather and site congestion. The City's brief was straightforward: open by July, durable finishes, public-facing program. We brought pre-fab to the conversation early — wall assemblies, roof panels, the bathroom cores. Built off-site, trucked in, set in two weeks.

Frame 02

The set. Wall assemblies arrived complete with rough electrical and exterior cladding. Crane scheduled for two days. Park closed for one weekend.

The schedule that ate the schedule.

Pre-fab compressed the on-site weeks but expanded the design weeks. Every wall section had to be production-ready six weeks before it landed. The architect lived in our office for the first month.

Once the design was locked, the build moved fast. The whole fieldhouse went up in 16 weeks. League soccer kept playing.

Frame 03

Open. First community event a week after handover. A soccer tournament. Families clustered under the gathering space. The building did its job.
