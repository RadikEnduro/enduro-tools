Dawson Lab

SectorResearch / Wet Lab
ClientUBC Faculty
Year2024
Duration18 weeks

Project Brief

Convert two adjacent wet-lab spaces into a unified research environment without disrupting active ops on the floors above and below.

The brief landed mid-summer with a hard September open. Two adjacent wet labs needed unification: a shared bench-line, fume-hood integration, gas + DI water rerun, and casework that matched the building's heritage millwork standard. The complication wasn't the spec — it was that the labs above and below would remain in operation throughout.

We phased the work in four blocks, ran the heavy demo after-hours, and isolated each zone with negative-pressure barriers tied to the building's HVAC. Mechanical and electrical leads coordinated daily. The lab closed on a Friday, opened on a Monday — 18 weeks later, with permits closed and the deficiency walk done before the invoice arrived.

It's the kind of build that proves the operating standard: live-ops awareness, schedule rigor, and the discipline to keep the work invisible until handover.

"They built around our research operations for four months and we never lost a workday. The schedule they sent on day one was the schedule we got at handover."
— UBC Faculty Lead, Dawson Lab
