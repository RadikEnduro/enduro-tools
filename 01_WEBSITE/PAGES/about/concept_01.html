About Enduro

Builders. Not subcontractors.

A Vancouver-based commercial GC built around one premise: complex projects deserve one accountable team, not a relay race.

Chapter 01

The shape of the team came first.

We didn't start with a brand. We started with a question: what would a construction company look like if its operating standard wasn't a marketing line — if it actually showed up in the field, every week, in the version of the schedule that got shipped?

What we built was a team small enough to be honest, large enough to take on real complexity, and structured so that the person you talked to in the kickoff meeting is the same person walking the punch list.

Chapter 02

Then the work found us.

UBC came first. Lab fit-outs, classroom builds, research facilities — projects where coordination is the deliverable and "live operations" isn't a phrase you can hand-wave through.

"They build around running labs the way good restaurants run service. Quiet, fast, no spilling on the floor."

Then healthcare. Then institutional. Then specialty TIs. The throughline has always been the same: somebody else needed the work done correctly and they needed to keep operating while we did it.

Chapter 03

Now we hire for it.

The throughline is also how we hire. PMs who can read a Gantt chart and a room with equal fluency. Superintendents who'd rather call you with the question than guess.

Trade leads who know that the deficiency walk happens before invoicing, not after — and that the version of the schedule that goes to the client is the version we hold ourselves to.

Operating principles.

01

One accountable team

Same PM, super, and crew lead from kickoff to handover. No mid-build handoffs. No "let me check with the office."

02

Open book on budget

You see the numbers we see. Change orders explained, not surprised. The version of the budget on Wednesday is the version on Friday.

03

Schedule as a commitment

The critical path goes on paper before the first wall comes out. We hold ourselves to the schedule we negotiated.

04

Live operations awareness

We build around running labs, clinics, and classrooms — phasing, isolation, after-hours work, infection control. The work doesn't disturb the work.

05

Quiet during, finished after

We over-coordinate so the project goes quiet on site. The right time to talk to us is at the front end and at handover.
