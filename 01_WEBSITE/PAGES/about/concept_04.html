Operating Manifesto · 2026 Doc / 002 · Public

We build like the schedule is a contract.

01

One team.

The PM in the kickoff is the PM at handover. The superintendent who quoted the schedule walks the punch list. No relay race. No "let me check with the office."

02

Open book.

You see the numbers we see. Change orders are explained, not surprised. The version of the budget on Wednesday is the version on Friday.

03

Quiet during, finished after.

We over-coordinate so the project goes quiet on site. The right time to talk to us is at the front end and at handover — the middle should be invisible.

04

Live operations are sacred.

We build around running labs, clinics, classrooms — phasing, isolation, infection control, after-hours work. The work doesn't disturb the work.

05

Deficiency walks before invoicing.

The list is closed before the invoice arrives. Not "we'll get to it." Closed. The handover is the work, not the postscript.

06

Hire for the throughline.

PMs who read a Gantt and a room with equal fluency. Supers who'd rather call you with the question than guess. Crews who know the schedule is the version they're held to.

Builders.
Not subcontractors.

If a project needs one accountable team, you've already met us. Send the drawings.
